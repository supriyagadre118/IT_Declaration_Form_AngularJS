Deduction under Chapter VI (Section 80C)

Maximum Limit Rs. 1 Lac. Additional tax benefit is available if funds are invested u/s 80CCG.

  • National Savings Certificate (NSC) is a 6-Yr small savings instrument eligible for section 80C tax benefit. The interest accrued every year is liable to tax (i.e., to be included in your taxable income) but the interest is also deemed to be reinvested and thus eligible for section 80C deduction.

  • Maximum limit Rs. 1 Lac

  • The amount you pay as stamp duty when you buy a house, and the amount you pay for the registration of the documents of the house can be claimed as deduction under section 80C in the year of purchase of the house.

  • Any amount that you pay towards life insurance premium for yourself, your spouse or your children can also be included in Section 80C deduction. If you are paying premium for more than one insurance policy, all the premiums can be included.

  • here are some mutual fund (MF) schemes specially created for offering you tax savings, and these are called Equity Linked Savings Scheme, or ELSS. The investments that you make in ELSS are eligible for deduction under Sec 80C.

  • Max 50000/- investment allowed under this section. Eligibility - The deduction under the Scheme shall be available to a NEW RETAIL investor who complies with the conditions of the Scheme and whose gross total income for the financial year in which the investment is made under the Scheme is less than or equal to TEN LAKH rupees.
